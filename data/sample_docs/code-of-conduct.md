# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The Code of Conduct sets out the standards of behaviour expected from every employee, contractor, and vendor working with or on behalf of the company. Adherence to this code is a condition of employment.

## Core Principles

1. **Integrity** — Act honestly and transparently in all business dealings
2. **Respect** — Treat every person with dignity regardless of role, background, or opinion
3. **Accountability** — Take responsibility for your actions and their consequences
4. **Confidentiality** — Protect sensitive company and client information at all times

## Workplace Behaviour

Employees are expected to:

- Maintain a professional, inclusive, and respectful environment
- Communicate constructively and avoid personal attacks in any medium (in-person, email, Slack, etc.)
- Respect differing viewpoints and challenge ideas, not individuals
- Arrive on time for meetings and meet agreed deadlines

## Harassment and Discrimination

The company has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, bullying, or discrimination on any basis, including but not limited to:

- Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Race, ethnicity, nationality, or religion
- Age, disability, or marital status

Any employee found to have engaged in harassment or discrimination will face disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any personal, financial, or professional relationship that could create a conflict of interest with their duties. Disclosures should be made to your line manager and HR in writing.

## Confidentiality and Data Privacy

- Do not share company financial data, client information, product roadmaps, or personnel records with unauthorised parties
- Do not use company data for personal gain
- Comply with the company's Data Protection Policy at all times
- Refer to the IT Security Guidelines for rules on data handling on devices and systems

## Use of Company Resources

Company equipment, systems, and time are for business purposes. Limited personal use is acceptable if it does not interfere with work or violate any other policy. Accessing inappropriate content on company devices or networks is strictly prohibited.

## Social Media

Employees must not post confidential company information, make defamatory statements about the company or colleagues, or represent personal opinions as the company's position on any public platform.

## Reporting Violations

If you observe or experience a violation of this code:

1. Report to your line manager (if not involved)
2. Contact HR directly at **hr@company.internal**
3. Use the anonymous whistleblower channel at **ethics-hotline.internal.com**

All reports will be treated confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited and is itself a disciplinary offence.

## Consequences

Violations of this code may result in a formal warning, suspension, or termination depending on severity. Serious breaches may also be reported to relevant regulatory or law enforcement authorities.
