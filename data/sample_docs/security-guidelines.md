# IT Security Guidelines

## Overview

All employees are responsible for protecting company data and systems. These guidelines are mandatory and apply to all staff using company devices, accounts, or networks.

## Passwords

- Minimum length: **12 characters**
- Must include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and a special character
- Do not reuse the last 10 passwords
- Do not share passwords with anyone, including IT staff
- Change your password immediately if you suspect it has been compromised
- Use the company-approved password manager (**1Password**) — contact IT for access

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is mandatory for all company accounts including email, VPN, HR Portal, and IT Portal. Refer to the **Employee Onboarding Guide** for setup instructions. Disabling MFA on any company account is prohibited.

## Device Security

- Lock your screen when stepping away (Windows: **Win + L**, Mac: **Cmd + Ctrl + Q**)
- Enable full-disk encryption (BitLocker on Windows, FileVault on Mac) — IT will confirm this is active on your device
- Do not install software not approved via the IT Software Request Policy
- Keep your operating system and company software up to date — do not dismiss update notifications for more than 48 hours

## Data Handling

- Store all work files in approved locations: **OneDrive**, **SharePoint**, or company servers — not local desktop or Downloads folder
- Do not copy sensitive data to personal USB drives or personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.)
- Classify documents according to the sensitivity labels in Microsoft 365: **Public**, **Internal**, **Confidential**, **Highly Confidential**
- Do not email **Confidential** or **Highly Confidential** documents to external parties without encryption

## Email and Phishing

- Do not click links or download attachments from unexpected or suspicious emails
- Verify sender identity before responding to requests involving financial transactions, credentials, or personal data
- Report suspicious emails using the **Report Phishing** button in Outlook or forward to **security@company.internal**
- IT will never ask for your password via email or phone

## Network Security

- Always connect via **VPN (Cisco AnyConnect)** when accessing internal systems from outside the office
- Do not use public Wi-Fi (cafes, airports, hotels) without VPN active
- Do not connect personal devices to the internal company Wi-Fi network — use the guest network instead

## Incident Reporting

Report security incidents immediately. A security incident includes:

- Lost or stolen device
- Suspected phishing or malware
- Unauthorised access to your accounts
- Accidental disclosure of sensitive data

Report to: **security@company.internal** or call the IT Helpdesk at extension **4000** (available 24/7 for security incidents).

**Do not attempt to investigate or resolve a security incident yourself.**

## Consequences

Violation of these guidelines may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. Serious breaches may be reported to regulatory authorities.
